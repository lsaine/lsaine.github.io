Grab Some Caffeine and Pull Up a Chair


While San Francisco's North Beach is no longer the solidly Italian neighborhood it once was, a strong Florentine heartbeat still enlivens its thriving cafe society. Every cafe differs from the next--in clientele and in temperament--and it's been said that the cafe you choose as "yours" says a lot about the kind of North Beacher you are.

The characters in these 13 stories live their lives out of the Caffé Trieste. They are fictional but the Trieste is very real. It claims to be the oldest coffee house on the West Coast and probably it is. But for its real-life habitués what counts is its warm embrace of the idiosyncratic, the iconoclastic, the square pegs defying the round holes.

As the city around it grows harder and less tolerant, seduced by those old harlots Greed and Avarice, the Caffé Trieste offers a sanctuary for those wayward souls threatened with extinction by the changing scene.

These are their North Beach Stories.
